The Spices are Sacred

Jerk cooking originated with the Maroons — escaped enslaved Africans who fled to Jamaica’s mountains. They combined African cooking traditions with indigenous Taino techniques to create jerk as a form of cultural preservation and survival. Jamaican Jerk Chicken is a bold and spicy dish from Jamaica, made by marinating chicken in a mix of hot peppers, spices, and herbs, then grilling it for that smoky, charred flavor. "Jerk" refers to the cooking style and the spicy seasoning — it's full of heat, sweetness, and island vibes.

What You Need!

  • 1.5 to 2 lbs chicken (thighs, drumsticks, or breast)
  • 2 tsp allspice
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1.5 tsp brown sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oil

How To Make It!

Preparation

Marinade:

  • Blend or mix all ingredients (except chicken) to make a marinade. You can use a food processor or do it by hand.
  • Marinate chicken in the mixture for at least 2 hours (overnight is best!).

Cook the chicken:

  • Grill, bake, or pan-fry:
    • Grill: Medium-high heat, 6–8 mins per side
    • Oven: 400°F (200°C) for 35–45 mins
    • Pan: Medium heat, brown until fully cooked through
