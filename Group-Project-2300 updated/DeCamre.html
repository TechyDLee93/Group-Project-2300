USA: "Biscuits and Gravy

Biscuits and Gravy is a classic Southern U.S. breakfast dish.
It’s made with fluffy biscuits smothered in creamy sausage gravy. The gravy is rich, peppery, and usually made from pork sausage, flour, and milk.
It’s cozy, hearty, and super satisfying!

Ingredients:


2 cups + 2 tbsp all-purpose flour,
1 tbsp baking powder, ½ tsp salt,
1 stick cold butter, ¾ cup buttermilk,
½ lb pork sausage, 2 cups milk,
salt (to taste), black pepper (to taste)

Preparation:


Making the biscuits:
Preheat oven to 425°F.
Mix flour (minus 2 tbsp), baking powder, and salt.
Cut in butter until crumbly.
Add buttermilk and stir into a dough.
Roll out, cut into circles, and bake on a tray for 12–15 minutes until golden.
Making the Gravy: In a pan, cook sausage until browned.
Break it up as it cooks.
Sprinkle in flour, stir for 1–2 minutes (this makes a roux).
Slowly add milk while stirring.
Let it simmer until thick. Season with salt and lots of black pepper.

Spoon gravy over warm biscuits and serve!
