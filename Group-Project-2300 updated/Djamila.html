About the Dish - Tô

Tô is a staple in Burkinabé cuisine, deeply rooted in the country's agricultural heritage. This dish is a thick porridge made from millet, sorghum, or maize flour, reflecting the grains predominantly cultivated in the region. Tô is typically served with a variety of sauces prepared from vegetables, meats, or fish, making it a versatile and nourishing meal. Its preparation and communal consumption underscore the importance of togetherness in Burkinabé culture.

Ingredients

  • Millet, sorghum, or maize flour
  • Water
  • Salt (optional)

Preparation

  • Boil water in a large pot.
  • Gradually add the chosen flour, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  • Cook and stir until the mixture thickens to a dough-like consistency.
  • Serve with a preferred sauce, such as vegetable stew or meat-based gravy.
