About the Dish - Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is a rich and creamy North Indian dish made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and a small amount of kidney beans (rajma). The name "makhani" means "buttery", and the dish is known for its velvety texture, deep flavor, and slow-cooked goodness. It's simmered in spices, tomatoes, and butter or cream, giving it that signature luxurious taste. This dish is a beloved comfort food in many Indian households.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz black lentils
  • 2 tbsp kidney beans(optional)
  • 1 Large onion(chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes(blended)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chiles(chopped)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • salt(to taste)

Preparation

  1. Soak lentils (and rajma if using) overnight, or at least 6 hours.
  2. Cook lentils in a pressure cooker with 3-4 cups of water and salt to taste until soft (6-8 whistles).
  3. In a pan, add ghee, then add cumin seeds and onion. Cook until golden.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chiles. Cook for one minute more.
  5. Add tomatoes. Cook until soft.
  6. Add spices (chili powder, garam masala, salt). Mix well.
  7. Add cooked lentils to the pan. Stir and simmer for 15-20 minutes. You can add water if it's too thick for your liking.
  8. Add cream and stir. Cook 2 minutes more.
