蛋炒饭 (dàn chǎo fàn)

Traditional Chinese Egg Fried Rice

Breakdown: 蛋 (dàn): means "egg". 炒 (chǎo): means "stir-fry" or "fried". 饭 (fàn): means "rice" or "cooked rice

History

Egg fried rice in Chinese cuisine has roots tracing back to the Sui Dynasty (589-618 AD) in China, where it was a practical way to utilize leftover rice and other ingredients. This humble dish, born from a need to prevent food waste, evolved into a popular and diverse staple. The earliest fried rice dishes were likely made with millet or sorghum, but the modern version with eggs and rice became prominent during the Sui Dynasty.
