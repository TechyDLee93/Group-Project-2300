Italy: Pasta al Pomodoro

Pasta is the heart of Italian cuisine, with recipes dating back to ancient times. Pasta al Pomodoro (pasta with tomato sauce) is a staple dish, highlighting Italy’s love for fresh, high-quality ingredients. This dish represents the Mediterranean diet and the importance of simplicity in Italian cooking. It is commonly enjoyed as a comforting meal at home or as a quick yet delicious restaurant dish.

Ingredients

  • 250g (8.8 oz) spaghetti or other pasta
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g (14 oz) canned San Marzano tomatoes (or fresh, peeled tomatoes)
  • Salt & black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • 5–6 fresh basil leaves
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Preparation

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil salted water in a large pot and cook pasta according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant (about 30 seconds).
  4. Pour in the canned or fresh tomatoes, crushing them with a spoon.
  5. Simmer the sauce for about 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add salt, pepper, and sugar if needed.
  7. Drain the pasta and add it directly to the sauce.
  8. Toss everything together, allowing the flavors to blend for 2–3 minutes.
  9. Stir in fresh basil leaves.
  10. Serve hot, topped with Parmesan cheese if desired.

Pasta al Pomodoro is often paired with a simple green salad and a glass of Chianti or Pinot Grigio wine. It can be served as a first course (primo piatto) in a traditional Italian meal, followed by a meat or fish dish.
